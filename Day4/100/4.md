## Day 4 - Topics Summary

- For loops ✅

  - The most common loop in Swift is the `for` loop.
  - Results: 11/12

- While loops ✅

  - Swift also has a `while` loop.
  - Results: 10/12

- Repeat loops ✅

  - There's also a `repeat` loop which is identical to a `while` loop except the condition to check comes at the end.
  - Results: 11/12

- Exiting loops ✅

  - To exit a loop you must use the `break` keyword.
  - Results: 7/12

- Exiting multiple loops ✅

  - A loop inside a loop is called a nested loop.
  - It's common to want to break out of both the inner loop and the outer loop.
  - To exit part-way through a loop you need to give the outside loop a label then use `break [OUTER LOOP LABEL]` to exit both loops at the same time.
  - Results: 6/6

- Skipping items ✅

  - To skip items in a collection you `continue` keyword.
  - Results: 7/12

- Infinite loops ✅

  - Using `true` as your condition will result in an infinite loop.
  - Results: 11/12

- Looping: Summary ✅
  - Results: 9/12
